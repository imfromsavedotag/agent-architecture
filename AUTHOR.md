# Author

Ian Murdock is a systems thinker and founder, not a career software developer. He dabbled in coding thirty years ago and never went further — what he has done consistently is seek out complex domains where he could learn, synthesize, and build. That habit is probably why this methodology has a constitution rather than a README full of promises.

This methodology grew out of a year of hands-on work building save.ag — a knowledge platform for a domain where the information is fragmented, contested, and context-dependent. The AI coding tools available were capable, but using them well required discipline the tools did not supply. The discipline had to be designed, tested against real production work, and revised when it failed. What is in this repository is what survived that process.

Save.ag is being organized as a California Public Benefit Corporation. Its mission is to make knowledge about sustainable land stewardship genuinely accessible — not curated down to digestible simplicity, but structured so that someone who wants to understand the evidence can find it, and someone who wants a quick answer can get one, and neither experience undermines the other. The methodology in this repository was the production tooling behind that work. It is published separately because the structural discipline it represents is not specific to that domain.

The canonical explanation of why this exists is at [save.ag/why](https://save.ag/why).
